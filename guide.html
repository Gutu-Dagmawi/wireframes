System Architecture & Roles

DocuMed is a multi-branch clinic management system with 32 screens across 7 roles. Every user is scoped to a branch — they only see data, queues, and patients relevant to their assigned branch.

Receptionist 8 screens First point of contact. Manages check-ins, appointments, patient registration, document delivery, and suspended encounters. Does not access clinical data.
Finance Clerk 3 screens Processes all payments: card activation, lab orders, procedures, consultations. Auto-priced from admin — clerks confirm payment and select method only.
Nurse 3 screens Collects vitals, performs triage, administers nursing procedures (injections, dressings, nebulisation). Two queues: vitals and nursing orders.
Practitioner (MD) 7 screens Clinical core. Assesses patients, orders labs, writes prescriptions, creates procedure orders, can suspend and resume encounters. Three queues: initial, lab results, resumed.
Lab Technician 2 screens Processes in-house lab orders, records results, releases them to requesting practitioner or receptionist. Does not handle external referrals.
Administrator 8 screens System configuration: branches, users, fee schedules, service/lab catalogs, card validity settings. No clinical access.
Branch Scoping: All data is branch-scoped. A receptionist at Branch A cannot see Branch B's queues, patients, or payments. Admins can manage all branches.

Patient Journey Flows

There are two primary patient journeys through the system.

1. Clinical Visit (Practitioner Encounter)

The standard doctor-patient visit. The patient sees a practitioner for diagnosis and treatment.

Main Flow
Receptionist (check-in, select practitioner) → Finance (card activation payment) → Nurse (vitals & triage) → Practitioner (assessment, diagnosis, orders, prescriptions)
Lab Order Path (in-house)
Practitioner orders lab tests → Finance (lab payment) → Lab Tech processes specimen → Results released → Practitioner reviews
Lab Order Path (external)
Practitioner orders external test → Generates referral document → May suspend encounter → Patient goes to external lab → Returns days/weeks later with results → Receptionist activates resumption → Nurse (re-triage) → Practitioner resumes encounter
Order-Sheet Path (nursing procedures)
Practitioner orders procedures → Finance (procedure payment) → Nurse administers (injections, dressings, etc.) → Status reported to Practitioner
Encounter Finalized
Practitioner finalises → Output documents sent to Receptionist (Encounter Outputs) → Print & deliver to patient

2. Special Service (Walk-In, No Doctor)

For services that don't require a practitioner: blood pressure checks, blood sugar tests, injections, etc.

Main Flow
Receptionist (creates special service order) → Finance (service payment) → Nurse / Lab Tech (perform service or process specimen) → Receptionist (results routed back, print & deliver)
Result Routing Rule: "Order source = result destination." Practitioner-ordered results go back to the Practitioner. Receptionist-ordered special service results go back to the Receptionist.

Patient Encounter Practitioner

The most complex screen in the system. A tabbed interface for full clinical documentation during a patient visit.

  • Patient Profile Tab — longitudinal patient record (history, allergies, medications, past encounters). Editable within encounter context.
  • Encounter Form Tab — visit-specific SOAP documentation. Includes subjective (from nurse triage), objective (examination systems), assessment/diagnosis (ICD catalog), and plan/management. Lab Results and Procedure Status panels appear on click within their respective order tabs.
  • Lab Order Tab — order lab tests from the branch catalog. In-house vs. external referral modes. Previously submitted orders show clickable rows that expand to reveal full lab results with values, reference ranges, and abnormal flags.
  • Order-Sheet Tab — order nursing procedures. Previously submitted order-sheets show clickable rows that expand to reveal procedure status with nurse notes and completion times.
  • Prescription Tab — drug formulary search, dosage, frequency, duration. Generates printable prescription.

Key Actions

  • Finalise Encounter — reviews summary, closes the visit, sends outputs to receptionist.
  • Suspend Encounter — for external lab results taking days/weeks. Preserves all clinical data. Documents reason, required items, and resumption date limit. Sends record to receptionist's Suspended Encounters screen.
  • Create Referral — generates a formal referral letter for specialist care.
  • Schedule Follow-Up — books a return appointment pre-populated with patient data.

Encounter Lifecycle

An encounter is a single clinical visit between a patient and a practitioner. It has the following states:

In Consultation Active encounter. Practitioner is documenting findings, ordering tests, writing prescriptions.
Paused — Lab Wait Same-day pause while waiting for in-house lab results. Patient stays in the building. Timer pauses. Auto-resumes when results are released.
Suspended Multi-day pause for external lab results. Patient leaves the clinic. All clinical data preserved. Receptionist manages resumption.
Resumed Patient returns from suspension. Original encounter reopened with all prior data + new vitals from nurse re-triage.
Finalized Encounter complete. Output documents sent to receptionist. No further edits (read-only).
Historical (Read-Only) Past encounter viewed from the patient profile. All data is read-only with full audit trail.

Encounter Suspension & Resumption

When a practitioner orders an external lab test whose results may take days or weeks, the encounter can be suspended rather than finalized. This preserves the clinical thread.

Suspension (Practitioner)

  1. Practitioner clicks Suspend Encounter on the encounter screen.
  2. Documents: suspension reason, required items for resumption, resumption date limit (based on expected test turnaround), and clinical notes for when results arrive.
  3. Sets re-triage preference (default: unchecked/skip). If checked, the returning patient will go through nurse vitals before seeing the practitioner.
  4. Optionally releases current prescriptions to reception immediately ("partial outputs").
  5. Encounter status changes to Suspended. Record appears in the receptionist's Suspended Encounters screen.

Resumption Path 1: Walk-In

  1. Patient arrives with external results.
  2. Receptionist opens Suspended Encounters, clicks Activate Now.
  3. System checks card validity against the practitioner's declared resumption date limit.
  4. Within limit: no fee. Past limit: re-activation payment through Finance.
  5. Re-triage rules:
    • Within limit + re-triage requested → Nurse (vitals) → Practitioner queue
    • Within limit + no re-triage → directly to Practitioner queue
    • Past limit → mandatory re-triage (regardless of practitioner's setting) → Nurse → Practitioner queue
  6. Practitioner reopens the original encounter with all prior data intact.

Resumption Path 2: Appointment

  1. Receptionist schedules a return appointment linked to the suspended encounter.
  2. On the appointment date, check-in routes the patient to the Resumed Encounters queue (not Initial Assessment).
  3. Same card validity and re-triage rules apply.
Clinical Continuity: The resumed encounter is the same encounter — not a new one. Assessment, examination, orders, prescriptions, and the full clinical context are preserved as a single thread.
Re-Triage Default: Within the declared limit, re-triage is optional (practitioner's choice at suspension time, default: skip). Past the limit, re-triage is mandatory since the patient's condition may have changed significantly.

Card Activation & Validity

Every patient visit requires a card activation payment processed through Finance. The card has a validity period:

  • Normal visits: Card auto-deactivates after the admin-configured duration (default: 14 days).
  • Suspended encounters: The practitioner's resumption date limit overrides the system auto-deactivation. The card remains valid for resumption until the practitioner's declared date, even if the general window has passed.
  • After expiry: A re-activation fee is charged through Finance before the patient can re-enter the clinical flow.
Configuration: Card auto-deactivation duration is set in Admin → Branch Configuration → Fee Schedule → Card Validity Settings. Configurable per branch.

Pricing Model

All prices are admin-configured. Finance clerks see auto-populated prices and cannot modify amounts — they only confirm payment and select payment method.

  • System fees (card activation, consultation, appointment) — configured in Admin → Branch Configuration → Fee Schedule.
  • Service/procedure prices — configured in Admin → Master Data → Services/Procedures.
  • Lab test prices — configured in Admin → Master Data → Lab Tests.
  • Branch price overrides — optional per-branch overrides in Branch Configuration. If set, they take precedence over master data defaults.
